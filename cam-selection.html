How much surging are you willing to put up with in low gears / at low speeds?
That will indicate how much overlap you can tolerate.

How high of an RPM are you willing to tolerate before the engine makes real power?
That will indicate the intake valve closing angle.

How often are you willing to change valve springs?
That will indicate how much lift you should run.

"Influence" might be a better word than "indicate" but these are the primary factors in cam selection, and how they affect the cam specs. If you're not thinking / discussing in these terms you're basically just rolling the dice. Everybody has their own idea of what constitutes a good cam for a particular usage scenario.
